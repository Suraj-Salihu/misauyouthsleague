Our Story

The Misau Youths League was established to create a platform for young people to contribute meaningfully to national development. We believe in the potential of youths as catalysts for positive change in our communities.

Our organization brings together young minds from Dambam, Darazo, Ningi, Warji, Ganjuwa and Misau zones to collaborate on initiatives that drive social, economic, and educational progress.

With 20 dedicated members and growing, we are committed to creating opportunities for skill development, entrepreneurship, and community service.

Our Constitution

The guiding principles of Misau Youths League

PREAMBLE

We, the youths of Misau and its environs, desirous of uniting ourselves under one umbrella body to foster unity, progress, peaceful co-existence, educational advancement, political awareness, cultural preservation, and community development, do hereby enact and give ourselves this Constitution to guide our affairs and activities.

ARTICLE 1: NAME

MISAU YOUTHS LEAGUE {MYL}

ARTICLE 2: MOTTO

"Empowering Youth for National Development."

ARTICLE 3: AIMS & OBJECTIVES

  1. Promote peaceful coexistence among youths and communities in Bauchi Central and beyond.
  2. Encourage educational, cultural, and socio-economic development.
  3. Foster leadership, political participation, and civic responsibility among youths.
  4. Engage in community service, self-reliance projects, and skills acquisition initiatives.
  5. Promote awareness against social vices such as drug abuse, thuggery, and violence.
  6. Collaborate with government, NGOs, and private organizations for youth empowerment.
  7. Serve as a unifying platform for youths across Bauchi Central Zone.

ARTICLE 4: MEMBERSHIP

  1. Membership shall be open to all youths (male and female) of Misau and environs between the ages of 15–40 years.
  2. Every member must abide by the constitution of the League.
  3. Categories of membership shall include:
    • Ordinary Members
    • Executive Members
    • Patrons/Matron(s)
  4. Membership may be terminated by:
    • Written resignation
    • Expulsion for misconduct, anti-organizational activities, or criminal conviction

ARTICLE 12: DISSOLUTION

In the event of dissolution, all assets of the League shall be donated to charitable organizations within Misau Zone as decided by the General Assembly.
